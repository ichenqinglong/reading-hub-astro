Paragraph 1

Amanda had been asleep for two hours when the house went dark.

No hum of the refrigerator. No glow from the router. Just the August night pressing in through the windows, and the sound of her own breathing.

“Clay.” She shook him. “The power’s out.”

They lit candles and checked their phones—no signal. The kids stumbled downstairs in their sneakers, Archie complaining, Rose clutching a blanket. This was supposed to be a vacation, a week away from Brooklyn, from everything. Now the house felt like a box.

Then came the knock.

Paragraph 2

Amanda’s hands began to tremble.

Clay opened the door to find an older Black couple standing in the dark—G.H. Washington, who said he owned this house, and his wife Ruth. They had driven from Manhattan.

Something had happened there, G.H. said. The whole city had gone dark. People were beginning to scramble for gas, for food, for any way out. He and Ruth had managed to evacuate before the roads locked up.

“What kind of something?” Clay asked.

G.H. shook his head. “We don’t know yet. That’s the honest answer.”

Outside, the woods were silent. No distant traffic, no city glow on the horizon. Just stars, and the chaos of not knowing what came next.
